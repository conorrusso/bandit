Findings

  • FictiSoft explicitly trains AI/ML models on all customer data with no opt-out mechanism (Privacy Policy Section 3: 'We use your data to train and improve our AI models. There is no opt-out from model training.')
  • Customer data is retained indefinitely for model improvement and analytics (Privacy Policy Section 4: 'We may retain information indefinitely if we determine it has ongoing value for our analytics and modeling work')
  • AI scoring system used for high-impact decisions (employment, credit, insurance) without human review rights, bias testing, or audit access (Privacy Policy Section 3; DPA Section 5 explicitly denies audit rights)
  • Vendor explicitly requires waiver of GDPR Art. 22 human review rights: 'You acknowledge and agree that you waive any right to human review of automated decisions made about you' (Privacy Policy Section 3)
  • DPA contains no AI-specific clauses, subprocessor restrictions, or prohibitions on training use; subprocessor changes permitted without notice (DPA Sections 2–3)
  • Special category data (health, financial) collected from data brokers without documented lawful basis for GDPR Art. 9 processing (Privacy Policy Section 2)
  • Privacy Shield cited for EU transfers despite framework invalidation in 2020; no reference to Standard Contractual Clauses (Privacy Policy Section 6)
  • No fairness evaluation, bias mitigation, model card, or AI transparency report documented — fails to meet EU AI Act transparency requirements for high-risk system
  • Blanket prohibition on model training opt-out: 'There is no opt-out from model training' (Privacy Policy Section 3) — likely violation of GDPR Art. 21(1) right to object to processing for legitimate interests
  • Waiver of human review rights: 'You acknowledge and agree that you waive any right to human review of automated decisions made about you' — direct contradiction of GDPR Art. 22 right to human review for legally significant automated decisions
  • Indefinite retention for 'ongoing value': Section 4 permits indefinite retention for 'analytics and modeling work' — exceeds GDPR necessity principle (Art. 5(1)(e))
  • High-impact automated decision-making without safeguards: Scoring for 'employment decisions, credit assessments, insurance underwriting' (Section 3) triggers EU AI Act High-Risk requirements; no bias testing, fairness evaluation, human oversight, or audit rights documented
  • Special category data collection without lawful basis: Section 2 collects 'health-related data, financial information' from data brokers without stated lawful basis for Art. 9 processing
  • No subprocessor list or AI-specific vendor review: DPA Section 3 reserves right to engage subprocessors 'without prior notice' and does not maintain public list — customer cannot assess third-party AI vendor risks
  • Privacy Shield reliance: Section 6 cites discontinued Privacy Shield framework (invalidated 2020) — likely requires SCCs or other mechanism not stated
  • Overbroad data sharing: Section 5 reserves right to 'sell, rent, or otherwise transfer your personal information to third parties' for 'any third party we deem appropriate' with no commitment to equivalent protection
  • Indefinite policy amendment: Section 10 permits unilateral policy updates 'without prior notice' with continued use constituting acceptance — violates transparency principle for material changes
  • Training claim contradicts retention: Stating 'we use your data to train and improve our AI models' while retaining indefinitely 'for analytics and modeling work' indicates training data is not segregated from weights and is retained post-training
  • No model card, transparency report, or fairness documentation: Absence of standard AI governance artifacts for high-impact system
  • DPA lacks AI-specific safeguards: DPA Section 2 references 'purposes of processing' without itemizing AI/ML training as distinct purpose requiring separate consent or notice

PROPOSED AI-SPECIFIC DPA CLAUSE: '(a) FictiSoft shall not use Customer Personal Data to train, fine-tune, or improve any machine learning or AI models, including foundation models, without prior written consent separately obtained for each such use.
